Myer-Briggs (16 personalities test)

Shalimay’s personality type was Consul. These types are known for being sensitive, aim for win-win situations wherever possible, are good at connecting with others and have a strong sense of duty. In a team situation, this personality type prefers to discuss things at length where the result ideally benefit everyone. They are decisive and have strong opinions which are beneficial if a decision needs to be made with absolute certainty.

Big 5 test

Openness

Shalimay received a high score in openness. She has creative tendencies, is adventurous and intellectual

Conscientiousness

A mid-score. She can be very organised and determined but also able to be impulsive and easily side-tracked.

Extraversion

A high score in this section. Shalimay enjoys talking to people and gains energy from social interactions.

Agreeableness

A high score in this section. Shalimay has a tendency to cooperate rather than compete with others. She enjoys taking care of others and is easily forgiving and trusting.

Neuroticism

A low score in this area, she is unlikely to react with fear or anger or any other strong negative emotion. Shalimay is generally happy to shrug off misfortunes and get on with her day.

How Do I Take This Into Account When Forming A Team

When forming a team, this personality would be more than willing and gladly work with a variety of people and personality types. They would thrive mostly with a team with a set leader, set tasks and expectations. This kind of personality although very versatile, some considerations should be made such as making sure the team they are inserted into doesn’t have too many strong personalities clashing with one another that may disrupt order and processes. Ideally the team should be filled with similar personality types as they just want to complete what needs to get done and help one another to reach the end goal.
